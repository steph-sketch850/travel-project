Why the Pacific Northwest?

PNW, the region of many landscapes


The Pacific Northwest is a region in the United States that includes the states of Washington, Oregon, parts of Idaho and the Canadian province of British Columbia.


Oregon is one of the most geographically diverse states in the U.S., marked by volcanoes, dense forests, and high deserts. The state is also known for its abundant waterways, which include the Columbia River, the Snake River, and the Willamette River. Oregon's economy is largely agriculture-based, with a focus on sustainable practices and organic farming.


Washington is home to a diverse range of landscapes, including mountains, forests, and coastlines. The state is also known for its outdoor recreational opportunities, including hiking, skiing, and fishing. Washington's economy is driven by industries such as aerospace, technology, and agriculture.

Mount Rainier


Mount Rainier is a volcano located in the Cascade Range of Washington state. It is the highest mountain in the state and is an iconic symbol of the Pacific Northwest. The mountain is known for its glaciers, wildflowers, and diverse wildlife.


Address:

Mount Rainier National Park, Washington, USA


What I like about it

I love this place because it is a beautiful place to visit. The mountain is stunning and the views are breathtaking. There are many hiking trails to explore, ranging from easy walks to challenging climbs. The park is also home to a diverse range of wildlife, including bears, elk, and mountain goats.
